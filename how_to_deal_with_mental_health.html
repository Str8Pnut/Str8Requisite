Learn ways to offer care, respect, and encouragement to those who are struggling.

General Support Tips

  • Listen without judgment and let them talk at their own pace
  • Avoid giving advice unless asked — be present more than persuasive
  • Encourage seeking professional help, like a counselor or therapist
  • Educate yourself about their condition to better understand their perspective
  • Check in regularly and offer consistent support
  • Help with daily tasks or responsibilities when needed (e.g., meals, rides, scheduling)
  • Stay patient and calm even during emotionally difficult conversations
  • Respect their privacy and boundaries, while still being available
  • Let them know you care consistently, not just in moments of crisis

How to Calm Angry or Irritated Individuals

For Kids

  • Use a calm, soft voice to model the behavior you want to see
  • Offer a quiet space or a sensory toy to help them regulate
  • Validate their feelings: "I see you're upset, and that's okay."
  • Engage in a calming activity like coloring or slow breathing
  • Stay physically close without crowding them, showing they are not alone
  • Provide predictable routines to help them feel secure
  • Use visual aids or picture schedules to create clarity
  • Encourage the use of calm-down corners or calming jars
  • Play soft background music or nature sounds to reduce stimulation
  • Practice mindfulness exercises such as guided imagery with animals or stories

For Teens

  • Let them express themselves without interrupting or judging
  • Use open-ended questions to show you care, like “What’s going on for you right now?”
  • Offer a walk or physical activity to help reduce stress
  • Respect their space, but remain emotionally available
  • Help them identify their triggers and suggest healthy coping strategies
  • Model emotional regulation yourself by staying calm
  • Encourage journaling or creative outlets like drawing or music
  • Allow them to listen to their favorite calming playlists
  • Introduce breathing apps or calming videos specifically designed for teens
  • Use humor gently if appropriate — laughter can help break tension

For Adults

  • Maintain a calm tone and body language — avoid escalating
  • Ask if they’d like to talk or need space — respect their boundaries
  • Use grounding techniques: suggest deep breathing or naming things they see
  • Encourage seeking professional support if emotions remain overwhelming
  • Validate their feelings without minimizing them (e.g., “That sounds incredibly difficult”)
  • Be a consistent, non-judgmental presence during and after difficult moments
