Simple ways to support your mental wellness every day.

When to Seek Help

  • If you're feeling very sad, worried, or angry most days
  • If you're having trouble eating, sleeping, or focusing
  • If you're withdrawing from friends and things you enjoy
  • If you’re feeling hopeless or thinking about hurting yourself
  • Talk to a parent, teacher, doctor, or counselor — you’re not alone
  • Even if you don't know what your feeling

Tips for Teens & Kids

  • Talk to someone you trust when you're feeling down
  • Get active — sports, music, or creative play can lift your mood
  • Stay off screens before bedtime to sleep better
  • Be proud of your efforts, not just your results
  • Join clubs or groups to meet supportive friends
  • Write in a journal to express your thoughts and track your feelings
  • Practice deep breathing or mindfulness when you feel overwhelmed
  • Set small personal goals to build confidence and motivation
  • Limit time on social media and follow accounts that make you feel good
  • Create a playlist of music that makes you feel calm or happy

Tips for Adults

  • Make time for your own self-care, even in short bursts
  • Ask for support — you're not meant to do it all alone
  • Celebrate small wins and be gentle with yourself
  • Stay organized with checklists or weekly planners
  • Join parent groups or talk with other adults facing similar things
  • Create a morning or evening routine that includes quiet reflection or journaling
  • Practice gratitude by writing down three things you’re thankful for each day
  • Engage in physical activity that you enjoy — even a 10-minute walk helps
  • Use positive affirmations to start your day with intention
  • Set realistic goals and reward yourself when you reach them
  • Unplug from screens for 30 minutes daily to reset your mind

Helpful Mental Health Resources

Explore more ideas on how to grow and support your mental health from these trusted sources:

  • NAMI: Self-Improvement Tools for Mental Health – Offers education, peer support, and recovery resources for people living with mental illness.
  • MentalHealth.gov: Understanding Mental Health – A government guide on what mental health is, warning signs, and how to get help.
  • Mental Health America: Self-Help Tools and Worksheets – Free worksheets and screening tools to support emotional wellness.
  • Headspace: Guided Meditation & Mindfulness – App-based mindfulness and meditation sessions for stress, sleep, and focus.
  • Psych Central: Mental Health Resource List – A comprehensive guide to mental health tools, articles, and hotlines.
  • Mind UK: Everyday Living Mental Health Tips – Advice on everyday mental wellness, including managing stress and setting boundaries.
  • Calm App: Relaxation and Sleep Help – Guided meditations, bedtime stories, and music to improve sleep and relaxation.
  • Verywell Mind: Self-Care Strategies – Tips on developing healthy habits, managing anxiety, and practicing self-kindness.
  • Jack.org: Mental Health Resources for Youth – Youth-focused programs and educational content for promoting mental wellness among young people.
