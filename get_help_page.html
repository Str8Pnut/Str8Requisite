You're Not Alone: Get Help

Seeking help is a sign of strength. Whether you're feeling overwhelmed, stressed, anxious, or just not yourself, support is available and healing is possible.

Immediate Support

  • Call or Text 988: Suicide & Crisis Lifeline – available 24/7
  • Crisis Text Line: Text HOME to 741741
  • National Domestic Violence Hotline: 1-800-799-7233

Find a Therapist

Therapists are trained to help with a wide range of challenges. You can search by zip code, insurance provider, or specialty on platforms like:

  • Psychology Today
  • BetterHelp
  • Talkspace

Local Resources in West Virginia

If you are in West Virginia, here are some local resources that can provide support:

  • Help4WV: 1-844-HELP4WV (1-844-435-7498) – 24/7 helpline for mental health and substance use support. Visit help4wv.com
  • Prestera Center: Offers crisis intervention, counseling, and psychiatric services. Visit prestera.org
  • WV Department of Health and Human Resources (DHHR): Find local community mental health centers through dhhr.wv.gov
  • United Summit Center: Mental health services for North Central WV. Visit uscwv.org

Support for Single Parents in Ohio & West Virginia

Being a single parent in Ohio or West Virginia comes with its own set of challenges and strengths. Whether you're seeking support for mental health, housing, childcare, or basic needs, there are services available to help you thrive.

  • Ohio Family and Children First Councils: Local councils coordinate services for children and families across each Ohio county. Visit fcf.ohio.gov
  • WV 2-1-1 Community Resources: Call 211 or visit wv211.org for referrals to parenting support, housing, and financial aid.
  • Catholic Charities of West Virginia: Provides emergency assistance, parenting support, and counseling. Visit catholiccharitieswv.org
  • Action for Children (Ohio): Offers parenting classes, home visits, and fatherhood support programs in Central Ohio. Visit actionforchildren.org
  • Ohio Department of Job and Family Services: Offers cash assistance, childcare subsidies, and employment support. Visit jfs.ohio.gov
  • WV PATH (People's Access To Help): Apply for benefits like SNAP, Medicaid, and TANF. Visit wvpath.org
  • YWCA Charleston & Dayton: Offers supportive housing, counseling, and domestic violence programs. Visit ywcacharleston.org or ywcadayton.org
  • Help Me Grow Ohio / West Virginia Birth to Three: Free services for young children and parenting support. Visit helpmegrow.ohio.gov and dhhr.wv.gov/birth23
  • Local 211 Services: Dial 211 or visit 211.org to find regional resources like food banks, housing help, and parenting programs near you.

Don't underestimate the power of talking to someone you trust. Sharing what you’re feeling with a friend or loved one can provide comfort and open the door to further support.

Emergency Checklist

Consider preparing a physical checklist to help you stay safe and organized if you need to leave quickly:

  • Photo ID, social security cards, birth certificates
  • Emergency contacts and important phone numbers
  • Cash, credit/debit cards, checkbook
  • Medication and prescriptions
  • Keys (home, car, work)
  • Clothing and personal hygiene items
  • Legal documents (restraining orders, lease, insurance)
  • Comfort items for children (toys, blankets)
  • Phone charger or backup battery
  • Backup transportation options

Additional Safety Tips

  • Keep communication discreet: Use apps or messaging platforms with disappearing messages if you're being monitored.
  • Know your escape routes: Identify multiple exits in your home or building and practice leaving safely.
  • Secure important documents: Hide or store documents in a trusted location outside your home if possible.
  • Establish daily check-ins: Ask a friend or family member to check on you at regular times.
  • Avoid predictable routines: If you're at risk, try varying your daily activities and travel routes.
  • Use safe words with children: Teach them a word or phrase to use when they need help or if something is wrong.
  • Keep emergency cash: Have a small amount of money hidden for emergency use.
  • Plan transportation: Know the public transit options near you and have a contact for emergency pickup if needed.

If you suspect someone you know is in this kind of situation, checking in and offering nonjudgmental support can make a big difference.
